## Content (CARE: Accessible framing)

The preceding analysis establishes that both commitment and flexibility have value, but at different stages:

- **Early commitment** attracts resources but forecloses learning
- **Maintained flexibility** enables learning but may fail to attract resources

The practical question is one of timing: **When should founders shift from flexibility-preserving to commitment-making strategies?**

---

## The Doubly-Binded Problem

Mobility ventures are **doubly binded**:

| Pressure | Source | Implication |
|:---------|:-------|:------------|
| **Commitment** | Capital-intensive (vehicle development, deployment) | Forces early lock-in |
| **Flexibility** | Volatile environment (regulatory, safety, customer perception) | Requires adaptation |

This explains the high failure rate in mobility ventures.

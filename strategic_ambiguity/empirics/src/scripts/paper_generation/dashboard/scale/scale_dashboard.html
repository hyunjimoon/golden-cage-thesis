PART II: THE MECHANISM (How it works)
The Golden Cage
Money creates asset specificity. Increased investment leads to specialized assets, raising the cost of abandonment (AOC).
[Capital] -> [Assets] -> [Lock-in]
Strategies: Reliability vs. Commitment
Ghemawat (1991) defines Strategy as "Commitment" (irreversible choice). Reliability (Quality) is a necessity, but Commitment (Sticky Assets) is a choice.
"Money is not experimentation. Money is concrete."
Headline Cases
Tesla
Believer
Survived the "Production Hell" by sheer belief capital. Low V (Precision) in product, but High V (Ambiguity) in vision.
Better Place
Commitment Trap
Locked into battery swapping stations (High Asset Specificity) before verifying demand. Classic "Golden Cage".
Quibi
Murky Middle
Tried to be both "High Quality" (TV stars) and "High Tech" (Short form) but failed to engage either audience.
Investor Styles
Analyst Exemplar: Sequoia
Metrics-driven. "Show me the unit economics."
Preference: Precision (Paper N).
Believer Exemplar: Founders Fund
Thesis-driven. "What is the secret you know that others don't?"
Preference: Ambiguity (Paper U).

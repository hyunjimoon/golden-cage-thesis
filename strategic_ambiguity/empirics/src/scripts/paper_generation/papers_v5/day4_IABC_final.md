# Chapter 1: Introduction

## The Puzzle

Ventures raising more early-stage funding show lower subsequent growth:

$$\frac{dG}{dF} < 0$$

This contradicts the premise that capital enables experimentation.

## Decomposition

$$\frac{dG}{dF} = \underbrace{\frac{dG}{dA}}_{+} \times \underbrace{\frac{dA}{dF}}_{-} < 0$$

**Table 1: Variable Definitions**

| Var | Name | Definition |
|:---:|:-----|:-----------|
| F | Funding | log(early-stage capital) |
| G | Growth | total funding / early VC |
| B | Breadth | positioning scope (0-100) |
| R | Repositioning | B_T − B₀ (signed) |
| A | Absolute Repositioning | \|R\| |

**Figure 1**: Causal diagram showing F → A → G pathway.

# Chapter 2: Analysis

## 2.1 The Paradox

| Link | Finding | Evidence |
|:-----|:--------|:---------|
| **dG/dA > 0** | Movers outperform stayers | 18.0% vs 9.9% (1.82×) |
| **dA/dF < 0** | Funding suppresses movement | 1-SD F → 0.4-SD less A |

## 2.2 The Mechanism: Golden Cage

**Theorem**: TRAP ⟺ HHI > θ

Three stages:
1. **Capital requires commitment** → specific promises
2. **Commitment attracts believers** → homogeneity (HHI↑)
3. **Homogeneity blocks learning** → no champion for alternatives

*When everyone agrees, no one can learn.*

**Figure 2**: Golden Cage mechanism visualization.

# Chapter 3: Prescription

## 3.1 Sequence Matters

**Error Pattern**: Applying Q1 (Capitalize) before Q2 (Evaluate) and Q3 (Segment).

**Prescription**: Q2 → Q3 → Q1

| Step | Question | Action | Fine's Tools |
|:----:|:---------|:-------|:-------------|
| Q2 | How to build? | Validate operations | Evaluate, Automate, Collaborate |
| Q3 | Where to play? | Choose market | Segment, Replicate |
| Q1 | How to scale? | Capitalize last | Platformize, Capitalize |

**Figure 3**: Q2 → Q3 → Q1 sequence diagram.

## 3.2 Core Principle

> **Nail with flexibility, Scale with commitment.**

**Table 3: Fine's 10 Tools × Q Sequence**

| Phase | Tools | Flexibility | Commitment |
|:------|:------|:-----------:|:----------:|
| **NAIL** | Segment, Replicate, Collaborate, Automate, Evaluate | High | Low |
| **SCALE** | Acculturate, Professionalize, Processify, Platformize, Capitalize | Low | High |

## 3.3 Application Pattern

Mobility ventures trapped in High-F, Low-A configuration:
- Manufacturing + Data + Safety → require capital
- Capital → stakeholder lock-in → suppress flexibility
- 91% stayer ratio, 5% survival

**Escape**: Return to Q2 (Evaluate) before committing more capital.

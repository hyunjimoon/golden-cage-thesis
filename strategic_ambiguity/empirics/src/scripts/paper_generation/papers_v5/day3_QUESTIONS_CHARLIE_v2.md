## Question 3: "Repositioning Capacity" vs "Repositioning Capability"

**Background**: I currently use these terms in the thesis:

> *Thesis text (¶15)*: "In nascent environments, commitment should target **repositioning capability** rather than static position."

> *Thesis text (C1)*: "Fund **repositioning capacity**, not fixed positions."

**Theoretical Context**:
- **Teece's Dynamic Capabilities (1997, 2007)**: The firm's ability to "sense, seize, and transform"—reconfigure resources in response to changing environments. This is an *organizational skill* that can be developed.
- **Capacity** (common usage): The potential or room for something (like "production capacity")—a *stock* or *resource*.

| Term | Connotation | Fits With |
|:-----|:------------|:----------|
| Repositioning **Capability** | Skill/ability to reposition | Teece's dynamic capabilities tradition |
| Repositioning **Capacity** | Room/potential to reposition | Resource-based view, slack resources |

**Question**: Which term better describes what ventures should commit to?

| Option A | Option B |
|:---------|:---------|
| **"Capability" (Teecean)**: Use "repositioning capability" consistently. This frames repositioning as a *learnable organizational skill*—sensing market signals, seizing pivot opportunities, transforming operations. Aligns with dynamic capabilities literature. Implies: repositioning can be practiced and improved. | **"Capacity" (Resource-based)**: Use "repositioning capacity" consistently. This frames repositioning as *organizational slack*—financial runway, uncommitted resources, optionality. Aligns with real options and resource-based view. Implies: repositioning requires buffer/reserves. |

**Your choice**: A / B / Neither (please explain)

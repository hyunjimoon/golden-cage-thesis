## The Funding Paradox

The scientific approach to entrepreneurship frames founders as hypothesis-testers who gather evidence, update beliefs, and iterate toward product-market fit. Building prototypes, hiring talent, and accessing markets require funding. The standard prediction: more funding enables more experiments, faster learning, and better outcomes.

**Yet our data contradict this prediction.**

Analyzing **180,994 ventures** funded in 2021-2025:

$$\rho(G, E) = -0.196^{***} \quad \text{(p < 0.001)}$$

**More early funding correlates with worse outcomes.** This is the **Funding Paradox**.

## The Mechanism: Commitment Crystallization

| Company | Promise | V Level | Outcome |
|:--------|:--------|:-------:|:--------|
| **Better Place** | "Battery swaps in exactly 3 minutes" | Low (Precise) | Bankruptcy |
| **Tesla** | "Accelerate sustainable transportation" | High (Vague) | $800B+ |

The difference was not resources but **commitments**. Better Place's precision attracted believers but blocked pivoting. Tesla's ambiguity preserved optionality.

## The Chain Rule

$$\frac{dG}{dE} = \frac{dG}{dA} \cdot \frac{dA}{dE} = (+)(-) < 0$$

- **dG/dA > 0**: Movement drives growth (Movement Principle)
- **dA/dE < 0**: Funding constrains adaptation (Fund2Cage)
- **dG/dE < 0**: Net effect is negative (Funding Paradox)

## Â¶67. Why Direction Adds Value



Directional movement (Zoom In or Zoom Out) provides three benefits that horizontal movement lacks. First, trajectory coherence: each step builds on the previous, creating cumulative learning rather than restart costs. Second, stakeholder alignment: investors, employees, and customers understand where the venture is heading, reducing coordination failures. Third, identity formation: the venture develops a clear narrative about what it is becoming, which attracts resources and talent aligned with that trajectory.



Horizontal movers lack these benefits. Without trajectory, each pivot incurs restart costs. Without direction, stakeholders face coordination challenges. Without coherent identity, the venture struggles to attract aligned resources. The result: horizontal movement is necessary (better than stasis) but not sufficient (worse than directional movement).



## Â¶68. Measurement Limitations



Our vagueness measure captures positioning breadth but not positioning content. A venture moving from "healthcare AI" to "fintech AI" might show similar V scores despite significant repositioning. This creates measurement challenges for horizontal movers: we observe that their vagueness stayed constant, but we may underestimate the magnitude of their content changes.



Future research could address this limitation through topic modeling to detect category shifts, embedding-based measures of semantic distance between positioning statements, and qualitative analysis of positioning changes. For now, we acknowledge that horizontal movement represents a heterogeneous category that our vagueness-based measure imperfectly captures.

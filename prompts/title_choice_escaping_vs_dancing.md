# Title Choice Evaluation: "Escaping the Cage" vs "Dancing with the Cage"

## Context

I'm writing a PhD thesis about venture capital and startup strategy. The thesis introduces a concept called the **"Golden Cage"**:

> When startups raise funding, they must commit to specific strategies. These commitments attract investors who believe in those strategies—skeptics never invest. The resulting board contains only believers. When market signals suggest pivoting, no one advocates for change. The venture is trapped: not for lack of capital, but for lack of diverse perspectives.

The thesis has 6 chapters:
1. Introduction (establishes the paradox: more funding → worse outcomes)
2. Theory: The Golden Cage (explains the mechanism)
3. Data and Identification (operationalizes the theory)
4. Where the Cage Binds (empirical results showing when/where the cage is tightest)
5. **[THIS CHAPTER]** (prescriptive: what founders can do)
6. Conclusion

## The Chapter in Question

Chapter 5 provides **three design principles** for founders:
1. **Strategic Ambiguity**: Commit to direction, not destination (vision-level vs operational commitment)
2. **Balanced Growth**: Diagnose bottlenecks before scaling (market vs operations)
3. **Financial Vehicle**: Sequence funding sources to preserve flexibility longer

The key insight is that founders **cannot fully escape** the cage—it's structural to the funding process. Instead, they must **work within its constraints** while preserving adaptation capacity.

## Two Title Options

### Option A: "Escaping the Cage"
- **Pros**: Active, urgent, suggests agency and freedom
- **Cons**: Implies complete freedom is possible; may overpromise what the chapter delivers

### Option B: "Dancing with the Cage"
- **Pros**: Suggests nuance—working with constraints rather than eliminating them; implies ongoing relationship, not one-time escape; metaphorically richer (dance = partnership, rhythm, adaptation)
- **Cons**: Less immediately clear; "dancing" may seem too playful for academic writing

## The Core Tension

The thesis argues that the cage is **structural**, not motivational:
- Founders **cannot** simply "escape" by trying harder
- The cage forms through belief-based sorting that's inherent to fundraising
- The solutions involve **designing commitment structures** that preserve flexibility—not avoiding commitment entirely

This suggests the chapter is about **navigating** constraints, not **eliminating** them.

## Your Task

Evaluate which title better serves:
1. **Accuracy**: Which title more honestly represents what the chapter delivers?
2. **Academic tone**: Which is more appropriate for a PhD thesis?
3. **Memorability**: Which creates a stronger conceptual hook?
4. **Coherence**: Which better fits with the "Golden Cage" metaphor established in Chapter 2?

Please provide:
1. Your recommendation (Option A or B)
2. A brief rationale (2-3 sentences)
3. Any alternative title suggestions that might capture both the agency and the constraint
